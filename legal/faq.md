# Frequently Asked Questions

To add a question, add a `## ` heading and an answer below it.

## Do I need to trim my GPS file to just the race?

No. Upload your whole session — warmup and cooldown included. The system
automatically finds the fastest valid segment between the course's start and
finish lines and uses that as your time, so there's no need to edit your file
first.

## What file formats can I upload?

GPX, FIT, and CSV files are all supported, and you can import an activity
directly from Strava once you've connected your account. Export your activity
from Garmin Connect, Strava, Apple Fitness, or any GPS device.

## Why wasn't my time recorded?

Your track didn't cross the course's start and finish lines (or, for gate
courses, every gate in the right order and direction) as configured. The
upload page shows a diagnostic map of your recorded track against the course
lines so you can see what happened — usually the GPS wasn't recording through
one of the lines, or the activity went a different way than the course
expects.

## Are heart rate and cadence stored?

No. Heart rate and cadence are intentionally discarded when your file is
parsed, even if the source file contains them. Only position and time are kept.

## Why are different boat classes on the same leaderboard?

Every entry records its boat class, and the leaderboard can be filtered to a
single class. Comparing across classes — say a K1 to an 8+ — isn't meaningful,
so filter to your own class to see where you stand.

## Who can see my time?

It depends on the course's visibility. Public courses and their trials are
visible to anyone; private courses are visible only to their owner; group
courses are visible to that group's members. When you make a trial public the
Terms of Service note that your performance times may become visible to others.

## What's a group?

A group is whoever runs the trials — a club, a coaching squad, or just you.
Courses and time trials belong to a group, and the group's admins are the
people allowed to create and manage them. Most paddlers just join the group
running their event and upload; you only need to create a group if you want to
organise trials yourself.

## How do I run a time trial?

Create a group first (you become its admin), then create a course inside it and
open a time trial on that course. The "create a group" link is on the Groups
page. Until you have a group, the create-course and create-trial buttons stay
hidden — that's deliberate, so paddlers who just want to upload aren't shown
organiser controls.

## Why don't I see a "create trial" or "create course" button?

Creating courses and trials is for group admins. If you don't belong to a group
you can manage, you'll see a "create a group" prompt instead — create one to
become its admin, or ask an admin of an existing group to add you.

## How do I submit my result?

Open the trial page and upload your trace (file, URL, or Strava import). If the
trial is members-only, you need to be a member of the group running it first —
the upload page will show a "join this group to submit" link when that's the
case. Public trials accept anyone; invitational trials accept the people the
organiser invited.

## How do I join a group?

Open the group's page and use **Request to join** — an admin approves you —
unless the group is set to open, in which case you join instantly. Admins can
also send you an invitation by email, or share a join link that lets you join
in one click. An invite-only group can only be joined via an admin invitation.
